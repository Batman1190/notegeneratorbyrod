K1 Visa

A K1 visa is a type of visa that allows the fiancé of a U.S. citizen to enter the United States to get married. After entering the U.S., the couple must marry within 90 days. Once married, the fiancé can apply for a Green Card to live and work in the U.S. permanently.

K1 Visa Requirements

Documents required for a K1 visa usually include proof of your relationship, financial support evidence, and proof of U.S. citizenship for the petitioner.

K1 Visa Process

The K1 visa application process typically takes several months (8-15 months), but processing times can vary depending on the U.S. Embassy or Consulate handling your case.

Documents for K1 Visa

Documents required for a K1 visa usually include: Proof of your relationship: This can include photos together, travel records, communication logs. Evidence of meeting in person within the past two years is typically required. Financial support evidence: The U.S. petitioner must provide proof of their ability to financially support their fiancé(e). This includes submitting an Affidavit of Support (Form I-134), recent tax returns, pay stubs, or bank statements to show sufficient income or assets. Proof of U.S. citizenship for the petitioner: This could include a copy of the petitioner’s birth certificate, passport, or naturalization certificate to prove they are a U.S. citizen. These documents help ensure that the relationship is genuine and that the fiancé(e) will not become a financial burden on the U.S. government.
