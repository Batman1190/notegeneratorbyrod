K1 Visa

A K1 visa allows the fiancé of a U.S. citizen to enter the United States to get married. After entering the U.S., the couple must marry within 90 days. Once married, the fiancé can apply for a Green Card.

K1 Visa Requirements

The required documents for a K1 visa typically include proof of your relationship, financial support evidence, and proof of U.S. citizenship for the petitioner.

K1 Visa Process

The K1 visa application process typically takes several months (8-15 months), but processing times can vary.

NOA1 (Notice of Action 1)

NOA1 is the first official receipt notice issued by USCIS after receiving a petition, such as Form I-129F (for a fiancé(e) visa) or Form I-130 (for a spousal visa).

  • Purpose: Confirms that USCIS has received the petition and started processing.
  • Processing Time: Typically issued 1–3 weeks after submission.
  • Receipt Number: Includes a 13-character case number (e.g., WAC, LIN, MSC, or EAC followed by numbers).
  • Next Step: Wait for NOA2 (Approval Notice), which can take several months.

NOA1 does not indicate approval—it only acknowledges receipt and that the case is in progress.

Affidavit of Support (Form I-864)

A form that a petitioner submits to prove financial ability to support the beneficiary.

Alien Registration Number (A-Number)

A unique identifier assigned to non-U.S. citizens by USCIS.

Approval Notice (Form I-797)

A USCIS notice confirming the approval of a petition or application.
